The Challenge of Growing Models and Datasets

  • Increasing Evaluation Costs
    • Larger models and datasets raise the cost of evaluation
    • Search for more efficient LLM evaluation methods is ongoing

Flash-HELM (Perlitz et al. 2023)

  • Prioritizes Higher-Ranked Models
    • Reduces computational costs by focusing on top models
  • Limitations
    • Ignores lower-ranked models, important in safety benchmarks
    • Random subsampling may cause significant estimation errors

Anchor-Based Coreset Selection (Vivek et al. 2023)

  • Coresets Based on Model Confidence
    • Focuses on models’ confidence in selecting the correct class
  • Limitations
    • Lacks rigorous theory
    • Can be unreliable if correctness patterns are spurious

IRT-Based Approaches in Recent Work

  • Tiny Benchmarks (Polo et al. 2024)
    • Uses IRT to minimize examples needed for LLM evaluation
    • Reduces computational costs while maintaining accuracy
  • IRT for Leaderboard Rankings (Rodriguez et al. 2021)
    • Models item difficulty and discriminability to improve rankings
    • Focuses on static evaluations

Our Approach

  • Distinct from Static Evaluations
    • Introduces Amortized Calibration to reduce costs
    • Uses a Language Model for automated question generation
  • Adaptive and Scalable
    • Enables continuous monitoring as models evolve
    • Supports long-term evaluation beyond static benchmarks
